Sphero BB 8 Droid Review

Forget Kim Kardashian’s arse, this little BB-8 droid toy almost broke the internet when unveiled recently. Now we’ve had a play, we can see why.

Bringing what is fast becoming a favourite character of the yet to be released Star Wars: The Force Awakens film to life, the smartphone-controlled BB-8 droid can whizz around your living room, office or Tatooine homestead performing tricks, spreading messages, pestering pets, saving the galaxy and simply looking downright awesome.

 Sphero BB-8 Droid Features: This isn’t your average droid
[youtube youtube_id=“pSxTIcD8D58″ width=“560″ height=“315″]

Essentially a standard Sphero – a remote control ball filled with some clever robotics that propel it around – with a new paint job and a snazzy hat, this toy is very much a case of evolution over innovation.

That doesn’t mean its BB-8 inspired looks and charm aren’t a winner, though. Yes, it would be nice if the droid echoed its movie-bound counterpart’s dusty, matt finish as opposed to its cheapening shiny gloss look, but this is just a relatively minor grumble of an otherwise stunningly finished product.

If you’re expecting a film-echoing footie-sized robot though, prepare to be disappointed. This BB-8 playmate is more tennis ball sized, with his magnetic, detachable head closer to a walnut’s circumference.

©  Digital Spy

Connecting via Bluetooth, the droid, which offers an hour’s use per charge, can be controlled via your iOS or Android smartphone. Refuelling the little guy is simple too thanks to the wireless conductive charging pad that doubles as a display stand for when the novelty inevitably wears off.

Sphero BB-8 Droid Performance: „Tryin‘ to catch me ridin‘ dirty…“

©  Digital Spy

Awesome styling aside, how well does the BB-8 drone work? Well, brilliantly. Using the accompanying, free-to-download app, the ball is surprisingly simple to control.

Once calibrated – a near minute-long process that precedes each use – the droid can be sent scuttling off at a surprisingly rapid speed, controlled via a digital joystick. At times there can be a bit of lag between your thumb’s movements and the droid’s responses, but unless you’re betting your podracer on a big-stakes droid race, this is nothing to worry about.

©  Digital Spy

On hardwood floors the little ball can struggle for traction before skittering off, making sharp turns tricky. Head to a rug or carpeted floors, though, and that top speed comes tumbling down.

What we found best was building little race tracks in our mind – round the chair leg, over the rug, under the sofa and behind the lamp. This quickly helped us master the Sphero’s movements, and better enjoy the toy-turn-robot.

Sphero BB-8 Droid App: Adding the power of a Jedi

©  Digital Spy

Like the bedroom of a Cribs-starring celebrity, this is where the magic happens. Transforming the BB-8 from an adorable paperweight to a real-life plaything, the Android and iOS-friendly app offers far more than a simple means of control.

Tired of doing all the driving? You can set your droid companion to go out and patrol on his own, mapping your room in the process, or simply bellow commands at your robot underling.

Thanks to your synced smartphone, the BB-8 can hear your voice commands. Preceding a prompt to „run away“ with „OK, BB-8“ will see the droid scarper in a straight line, while the same prefix on a „look around“ command sees the adorable droid patrol keep an eye out for approaching Storm Troopers.

©  Digital Spy

The app provides detail diagnostics on your droid too, from top speeds and distances covered to core temperatures and impact forces when you ‚accidentally‘ send it off a ledge.

You can even send hologram messages, well, sort of. The app lets you record video messages, and replaying them when the phone’s camera is lined up on the droid sees them newly adorned with a blue-tinged hue and distorted holographic crackle.

It would be nice if there were some Star Wars inspired mini-games or missions to download to give our limited imaginations a helping hand. Further compounding this limited support, we’ve struggled getting our BB-8 to work with Sphero’s non-Star Wars themed apps.

Verdict

©  Digital Spy

Is this the droid you’re looking for? Well, if you don’t mind being light of wallet, the BB-8 droid is the ultimate in desirable Star Wars toys. Forget faux lightsabers and LEGO Millennium Falcons, this interactive plaything will kick sci-fi loving nerdgasms on to unparalleled levels.

The novelty factor with inevitably wear off – and probably pretty quickly – but then who doesn’t want their own BB-8 droid sat on their shelf acting as a constant reminder of their Star Wars love and former riches? If you can stomach the outlay, do it. You’ll have a lot of fun while you wait for the film to launch this December.
